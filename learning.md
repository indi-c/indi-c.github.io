# Welcome to my repository of free learning resources

## About

This is a proof of concept site created to help further the goals of the UN Sustainability Goal 4, which is to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education access and to promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Particularly, I have the aim to assist in target 4.4, which is to by 2030 increase the number of youth and adults who have relevant skills for decent jobs and entrepeneurship.

Additionally, I aim to assist in goal 8, the promotion of sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. The target here I aim to assist in is 8.5, which is to by 2030 achieve full and productive employment and decent work for all women and men, including young people and those who are disabled.

## Justification

It has been found that MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) are successful in bridging the gap between skills graduates have and the skills employers expect. (Calonge and Shah, 2016).

In addition, Hadavand, Gooding, and Leek (2018), found that free or inexpensive MOOCs are being used significantly by those with low incomes or those from low income countries and that they may be a positive resource for those unable to enroll in traditional training programs or higher education.

Many free courses provide shareable credentials. Athey and Palikot (2025) found a substantial estimated increase in employability in those with otherwise weak CVs (no degree, lack of experience) where a credential was shared in a job profile.

Software engineering, a good career path with high pay, is notably accessible to those who are self taught through free online resources. In 2022, for instance, it was found that in the UK only 51% of job listings for mobile application developers required a bachelor's degree. This is 23% lower than the number found in 2017. (Goel et al., 2023) This indicates that access to free resources for learning software engineering could be very beneficial to those aiming for decent high paid work.

While not all resources here are MOOCs, they still assist in the creation of portfolios for work and upskilling in a way that could have a significant impact. There is little research done on the efficacy of these other resources, unfortunately.
